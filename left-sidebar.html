Odyssee Courses

Unit Description Class
Geology The study of Rocks, Their processes, and their role on the world around us. Earth Science
Meteorology The study of the Earth's atmosphere, weather, and climate. Earth Science
Oceanography The study of the Earth's oceans, including their physical, geological, chemical, and biological aspects. Earth Science
Astronomy The study of the Earth's place in the universe, including the solar system, stars, and other celestial bodies. Earth Science
Environmental Science The study ecosystems, biogeochemical cycles, and natural resources Earth Science
Cell structure The fundamental components that make up a cell, including a plasma membrane, cytoplasm, DNA, and ribosomes Biology
Genetics The study of heredity, or how traits are passed from parents to children through DNA Biology
Evolution The process of change in living organisms over generations through genetic modifications Biology
Ecology The Relations of organisms to one another and to their physical surroundings. Biology
Matter and energy the study of Matter and Energy Chemistry
Atomic structure The Makeup of an Atom Chemistry
Chemical bonding Learn about the force that holds atoms, ions, and molecules together to form compounds and other structures. Chemistry
Stoichiometry the branch of chemistry that deals with the quantitative relationships between reactants and products in a chemical reaction Chemistry
Mechanics the branch of physics that studies the motion of objects and the forces that cause that motion Physics
Thermodynamics the branch of science that studies heat, temperature, and the relationship between heat and other forms of energy, such as work Physics
Electromagnetism Learn about electric fields, circuits, magnetic fields, electromagnetic induction, and the electromagnetic spectrum, which ranges from radio waves to gamma rays Physics
Optics the wave nature of light through phenomena like interference, diffraction, and polarization Physics
